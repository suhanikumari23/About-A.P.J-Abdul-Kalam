A.P.J Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Missile Man Of India

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam fondly remembered as the Missile Man of India, was a visionary scientist, teacher, and the 11th President of India. Born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he rose from humble beginnings to lead India’s missile and space programs through his work at ISRO and DRDO. He played a pivotal role in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests of 1998, strengthening India’s defense capabilities. Serving as the People’s President from 2002 to 2007, he inspired millions with his simplicity and dedication to youth empowerment. His books like Wings of Fire and Ignited Minds continue to motivate students toward science and nation-building. Honored with the Bharat Ratna in 1997, he passed away on 27 July 2015 while delivering a lecture, leaving behind a legacy of vision, humility, and inspiration.

Biographies

  • Wings of Fire – His autobiography, co-authored with Arun Tiwari, narrating his childhood, education, and journey as a scientist.
  • Birth & Childhood: Born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, into a modest family.
  • Education: Studied physics at St. Joseph’s College, Tiruchirappalli, and aeronautical engineering at Madras Institute of Technology.
  • ISRO Contributions: Directed India’s first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) that placed Rohini satellite in orbit (1980).
  • Missile Development: Led the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme (IGMDP), creating Agni and Prithvi missiles.
  • Nuclear Tests: Played a crucial role in Pokhran-II nuclear tests (1998), strengthening India’s defense capabilities.
  • Presidency: Served as the 11th President of India (2002–2007), widely known as the “People’s President.”
  • Vision 2020: Advocated for transforming India into a developed nation through science, technology, and innovation.
  • Books: Authored inspirational works like Wings of Fire, Ignited Minds, India 2020, and Indomitable Spirit.
  • Awards: Received Padma Bhushan (1981), Padma Vibhushan (1990), and Bharat Ratna (1997).
  • Legacy: Passed away on 27 July 2015 while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong; remembered as the “Missile Man” and a mentor to millions of students.
